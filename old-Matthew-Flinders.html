Cognitive Science in Practice

Teacher interviews and classroom lessons from Matthew Flinders Anglican College demonstrate how evidence-informed strategies grounded in Cognitive Science are influencing modern teaching and learning.
Each example highlights approaches such as retrieval practice, questioning, and explicit instruction — showing how these methods build engagement, deepen understanding, and support lasting learning.

Teacher Interviews

In these short interviews, teachers share how they’ve embedded Science of Learning principles in their classrooms — from structured lesson openers to questioning techniques that draw every student into the discussion. Together, these reflections reveal how intentional, evidence-based teaching can foster confidence, clarity, and curiosity among learners.

Lessons

These lessons capture Cognitive Science in action. Each classroom sequence shows how teachers structure learning through techniques like Do Now routines, retrieval practice, modelling, and deliberate questioning. The focus is on visible learning, shared attention, and active engagement — helping students to consolidate knowledge and build confidence over time.
