ISQ Curriculum Leaders Forum
Emporium Hotel, South Bank
21st October 2025

The final Curriculum Leaders Forum for 2025 brought educators together to explore how we can enable all learners to thrive. The keynote by Dr Amy Berry focused on student engagement — showing how teachers can help learners move from passive participation to taking ownership of their learning.

The event also featured an inspiring interview with Marlena Katene, who shared her lived experience and insights into inclusion, empowerment, and supporting every learner to participate fully.

Together, these sessions highlighted practical, evidence-informed approaches that help students build confidence, curiosity, and a genuine love of learning.

Interview: Marlena Katene

Marlena Katene reflects on her journey as a learner, advocate, and writer whose educational path was shaped by her mother’s determination that she be part of her local community. Rather than attending a segregated school, Marlena grew up surrounded by peers and teachers who helped her learn, connect, and thrive.

In the interview, she shares how genuine inclusion extends far beyond access — it’s about belief, respect, and the willingness to see every student’s potential. Her experiences remind us that the most powerful learning environments are built on empathy, adaptability, and shared humanity.

Marlena’s story challenges educators to rethink what inclusion truly means: not a policy or a program, but a mindset — one that transforms classrooms, strengthens communities, and shapes confident learners ready to take on the world.
